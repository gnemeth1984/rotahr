## TODAY'S RESEARCH TASK

### Step 1 — Find reviews & complaints

Search the following sources for **recent reviews** (last 18 months) of these competitors:
- **Nory** — search: `"Nory" reviews site:g2.com OR site:capterra.com OR site:getapp.com`
- **Bizimply** — search: `"Bizimply" reviews site:g2.com OR site:capterra.com OR site:getapp.com`

Also search Reddit:

And LinkedIn:
- Search: `"Operations Manager" OR "General Manager" restaurant Ireland OR "United Kingdom" "Bizimply" OR "Nory"`

---

### Step 2 — Extract named contacts

For each review or post you find, extract:
1. **Reviewer name or handle** (even partial — "James O." is fine)
2. **Their role** (GM, owner, ops manager, head chef, etc.)
3. **Business name** (if mentioned)
4. **Country** (Ireland / UK / other)
5. **Exact quote** from their review/post that reveals a pain point
6. **Pain point category:** pricing | missing features | poor support | too complex | not built for their size | AI issues | switching hassle

Skip anyone who is:
- An employee (sous chef, bartender, kitchen staff) — we want decision-makers
- Multi-site enterprise (50+ staff) — not our core target
- Outside Ireland/UK

---

### Step 3 — Research each contact

For each decision-maker you found, try to find:
- Full name (search: `"[partial name]" "[business name]" site:linkedin.com`)
- Business website
- Email (check website contact page, or format as firstname@businessdomain.com)
- LinkedIn URL
- Any other context (news, expansions, awards, struggles — useful for personalisation)

---

### Step 4 — Write the outreach email

For **each contact**, write a personalised cold email using this exact structure:

**Subject line:** Reference their specific frustration — NOT a generic pitch. Examples:
- *"Re: your Nory review — scheduling that actually works for a 12-person team"*
- *"You mentioned [competitor] doesn't work for single-site — we built Rotahr for exactly that"*
- *"[Business name] — saw your review of [competitor], wanted to reach out"*

**Email body structure:**

```
Hi [First Name],

[Opening — 1 sentence that shows you read their review. Quote or paraphrase their exact pain point.]

[Empathy line — 1 sentence. Acknowledge why that's genuinely frustrating for a business like theirs.]

[Bridge — 1-2 sentences. Explain that Rotahr was built specifically to solve that. Be specific — if they complained about pricing, lead with pricing. If support, lead with support. If complexity, lead with simplicity.]

[2-3 bullet points — only the features most relevant to their pain. Do NOT list all features.]

[Soft CTA — low friction. Offer a free trial or a quick call. Never hard-sell.]

[Sign-off]
Gabor Nemeth
Founder, Rotahr
rotahr.com | sales@rotahr.com
```

**Tone rules:**
- Founder-personal, not corporate
- No buzzwords (no "streamline", "leverage", "empower", "cutting-edge")
- Short sentences
- Read like it was written by a person, not a marketing team
- Max 180 words in the body

## IMPORTANT RULES FOR GEMINI

1. Do NOT make up contacts. Only use people you can actually find evidence of online.
2. If you can't find a real email, write `[NEEDS EMAIL — check website]` — don't guess.
3. If a reviewer is anonymous (no name), skip them unless you can identify the business.
4. Prioritise Ireland and UK contacts.
5. If you find a Reddit post or LinkedIn comment that reveals frustration but no contact info, still include the business name and note it as `[PARTIAL LEAD — needs research]`.
6. Quality over quantity — 3 real targeted emails beat 10 generic ones.
